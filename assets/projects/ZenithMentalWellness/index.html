Why Men's Mental Health Is Declining

Research-backed factors contributing to the mental health crisis among men.

Toxic Masculinity & Social Expectations

Traditional gender roles discourage emotional expression, creating internal pressure and preventing men from seeking help when needed.

Social Isolation & Loneliness

Modern lifestyle changes, remote work, and reduced face-to-face interactions have led to increased isolation and weakened social support networks.

Work-Life Pressure & Economic Stress

Increasing job demands, financial pressures, and the expectation to be primary providers create chronic stress and burnout.

Digital Age & Social Media Impact

Constant comparison, information overload, and reduced genuine human connection through social media contribute to anxiety and depression.
